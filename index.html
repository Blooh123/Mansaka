Who Are the Mansaka?

The Mansaka are one of the 18 indigenous ethnolinguistic Lumad groups in Mindanao and the most numerous indigenous group in Compostela Valley and nearby areas. They inhabit the mountainous regions of Davao de Oro (formerly Compostela Valley) in the Philippines.

Even though many Mansaka are now Christians, they still embrace much of their traditional beliefs, culture, and identity. Their name derives from "man" meaning "first" and "saka" meaning "to ascend" — together meaning "the first people to ascend the mountains" or "first upstream."

Cultural Highlights

Mountain Heritage

Ancestral origin linked to Mainit Hot Spring, birthplace of the first Mansaka. Deep connection to highland ecosystems and ancestral lands.

Spiritual Wisdom

Led by traditional spiritual leaders called Baylan who serve as healers and mediators with the spiritual realm and supreme being, Magbabaya.

Distinctive Culture

Geometric designs in traditional clothing, shell and wooden ornaments, and distinctive embroidery patterns that identify their heritage.

Resilient Community

Strong sense of pride among generations to preserve identity, culture, and heritage despite modern challenges and environmental pressures.
