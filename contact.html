Frequently Asked Questions

How can I use Mansaka cultural materials for educational purposes?

You can freely use our educational materials for classroom teaching with proper attribution. For specific research or publication, please contact us for permission and citation guidelines.

Can I submit my own research or photographs?

Yes! We welcome research contributions, photographs, videos, and documentation from researchers, artists, and community members. Please contact us through the submission email with details about your materials.

How can I learn the Mansaka language?

We provide basic language resources on our language page. For comprehensive learning, contact local Mansaka communities or cultural organizations that offer language classes and immersion programs.

Is there an organization working on Mansaka cultural preservation?

Yes, several organizations work on indigenous rights and cultural preservation in the Philippines. Contact us for information about local community organizations and national initiatives.

Can groups visit or learn directly from Mansaka communities?

Cultural visits and educational exchanges can be arranged respectfully. Please contact us to discuss your group's interests, and we can facilitate proper introductions and protocols.

How can I support Mansaka cultural preservation efforts?

You can support through education, sharing our resources, making donations to cultural organizations, engaging respectfully with communities, and advocating for indigenous rights and cultural preservation.
