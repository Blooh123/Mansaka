Livelihood & Economy

Traditional Practices and Modern Challenges

Traditional Subsistence Farming

Centuries of Agricultural Heritage

For centuries, the Mansaka practiced shifting agriculture as their primary livelihood. This sustainable farming method, adapted to mountainous terrain, allowed them to live in harmony with their environment while meeting their food and economic needs.

Traditional Crops

The Mansaka cultivated a diverse range of crops suited to highland conditions:

  • Upland Rice: The staple grain crop
  • Corn (Maize): Important for both food and trade
  • Root Crops: Including camotes (sweet potatoes) and other tubers
  • Vegetables: Seasonal varieties adapted to local climate
  • Fruits: Native and cultivated varieties
  • Cash Crops: Coffee and abaca for market trade

Sustainable Practices

Traditional Mansaka agriculture incorporated conservation practices that maintained soil fertility, protected forests, and preserved biodiversity. These practices demonstrate sophisticated ecological knowledge developed over generations.

The Shift to Mining: Opportunities and Challenges

Mining as New Livelihood (1970s Onward)

Since the 1970s, mining—especially gold mining—has become an increasingly important source of livelihood for many Mansaka families. The Compostela Valley region is rich in copper and gold ore, making it an attractive area for mining operations.

Economic Opportunities

Mining income has provided new economic possibilities for Mansaka communities:

  • Increased household income compared to traditional farming
  • Ability to send children to schools in urban areas like Tagum City
  • Access to better healthcare and social services
  • Employment opportunities for young adults

Environmental and Social Costs

However, mining activities have created significant challenges:

  • Impact on ancestral lands and sacred sites
  • Pollution of local rivers and water sources
  • Deforestation and habitat destruction
  • Health risks for mining workers and nearby communities
  • Displacement of traditional farming communities

Modern Livelihood: Blending Tradition and Progress

Diverse Economic Activities

Agriculture

Many Mansaka continue farming, growing traditional crops and cash crops. Some families combine farming with other income sources.

Mining

Gold and copper mining remains significant, though concerns about sustainability and environmental impact persist.

Urban Employment

Mansaka professionals work in Tagum City and other urban centers in education, healthcare, business, and government sectors.

Crafts & Arts

Traditional crafts including weaving, carving, and beadwork provide income while preserving cultural heritage.

Education & Culture

Community leaders work to preserve Mansaka language, traditions, and knowledge through educational programs.

Cultural Tourism

Growing interest in indigenous culture creates opportunities for cultural tourism and heritage-based enterprises.

Challenges, Resilience & Future Outlook

Environmental Degradation

Deforestation, landslides, and mining impacts have degraded the ancestral domain. Some municipalities were declared uninhabitable after severe damage.

Cultural Pressure

Rapid modernization creates tension between preserving traditions and adapting to modern society, especially among younger generations.

Land Rights

Despite certified ancestral domain status, Mansaka face challenges protecting their lands from exploitation and development.

Innovation & Adaptation

Mansaka communities are developing sustainable alternatives to mining and exploring eco-friendly economic models for future generations.

A Strong Spirit of Resilience

Despite economic hardships and environmental challenges, there is a strong sense of pride among the Mansaka—including younger generations—to preserve their identity, culture, and heritage. Many recognize that true wealth lies in their connection to the land, their cultural practices, and their community bonds.

Looking Forward: The Mansaka continue to seek sustainable development paths that honor their heritage, protect their environment, and create opportunities for future generations while maintaining their cultural identity and connection to ancestral lands.

About Mansaka Heritage

This website is dedicated to preserving and promoting the cultural heritage of the Mansaka Indigenous Group of the Philippines. We aim to educate, inform, and inspire cultural appreciation.
